# Discrete Variables

Describe what a categorical variable is and provide an example of one.

## Requirements
- The student must correctly define a categorical variable: a variable with discrete, finite, values, possibly non-numeric.
- The student must provide an example of a categorical variable.

## Hints
- Think about what type of variable a firm's industry is.
- Consider how you would record a firm's headquarters state in an excel spreadsheet

## Solution Explanation
A categorical variables is one that has discrete values, like categories, types, or groups. Examples include industry, state, or the tone of a text (positive, negative, neutral), but could also be "bins" of a continuous variable, like size deciles, or above/below median value. These are typically what classifiers are meant to predict.
