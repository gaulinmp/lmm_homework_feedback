# Continuous Variables

Describe what a continuous variable is, and provide an example of one.

## Requirements
- The student must correctly define a continuous variable: a numeric variable with continuous (e.g. smooth) values.
- The student must provide an example of a continuous variable.

## Hints
- Think about what type of variable "Revenue" is.
- Consider the difference between a variable measuring Revenue and one denoting Industry.

## Solution Explanation
A continuous variable is one that takes a continuous value, as a measure of some quantity. 

Examples include Revenue, the latitude and longitude of a firm's branches, or time. These are typically what regressions are meant to predict.
